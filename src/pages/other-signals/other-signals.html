Other Signals

Suspending play (time) and dead ball

  • Any umpire may suspend play by calling 'time'.

  • Call is made loud and clear while raising both arms above the head.

  • Calling 'time' will immediately suspend play, so it is important to wait until all plays have finished.

  • The same signal is used for dead ball situations, such as when the pitched or batted ball strikes the batter's body. In this case the call is 'dead ball'

Suspending play (time) and dead ball

  • The home run signal is given when a fair-batted fly ball travels over the outfield fence prior to touching the ground, or hits the foul pole above the fence line.

  • The right arm is extended above the head with the fist clenched. A clockwise circling motion is used to indicate the home run.

Suspending play (time) and dead ball

  • Rather than suspending play, it is sometimes necessary to simply stop the pitcher from pitching when the batter is not ready.

  • The plate umpire ‘stops the pitcher’ by raising their open right hand (or left hand for a left handed batter) and pointing their palm toward the pitcher to gain their attention.
