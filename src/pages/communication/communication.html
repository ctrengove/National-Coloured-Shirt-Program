Communication

The plate meeting

Just before the game starts, a plate meeting is held between the umpires and the coaches of both teams.

The plate umpire stands behind home plate, the base umpire stands in front of the plate, and the coaches stand to the sides, on the same side as the dugout that their team is occupying.

The plate meeting provides the opportunity to make introductions, to clarify any special rules or game conditions, and to confirm team line-ups.

Giving the count

The count should be given

  • After three pitches, and when three balls and/or two strikes is reached

  • After placing the ball into play after a delay or suspension in play

  • When a base runner advances to the next base

Note: When giving the count, it is verbalised, with balls first, and strikes second. The number of balls is indicated with the left hand, and the number of strikes is indicated with the right.
