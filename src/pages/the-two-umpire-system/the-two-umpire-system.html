The Two Umpire System

Beginning umpires commonly start out as a base umpire working with a plate umpire in the Two Umpire System. This System divides responsibility for various decisions between the two umpires.

  • The plate umpire calls balls and strikes, safes and outs, fair and foul and plays at 3rd base and home

  • The base umpire calls safes and outs on plays at 1st and 2nd base

  • Both umpires must watch runners touch bases and base runners tagging up on fly ball

Note: The Two Umpire System requires umpires to move into positions appropriate for each play.

Base umpire positioning and movement

The starting position for the base umpire when there are no runners on base is in foul territory about 6 or 7 metres beyond 1st base.

With a runner on 1st, the starting position for the base umpire is half to two-thirds of the distance between 1st and 2nd base.

With runners on 2nd or 3rd, the base umpire starts approximately halfway between 2nd and 3rd bases.

  • For an infield play, the umpire moves into fair territory and obtains an angle on the play

  • For an outfield hit, the umpire moves into the diamond, keeping the ball and the play in front of you
